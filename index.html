How the timing calculator works

The tool helps you see whether last night’s timing creates a stable morning or a chaotic one.

  1. Enter last night’s timing: dinner/snacks, last liquids, magnesium time, sleep/wake.
  2. Add context: sugar, electrolytes, fiber, stress, exercise.
  3. Get your Morning Stability Score (0–100) and log how your gut actually behaved.

Who this is for

  • People whose IBS or visceral hypersensitivity hits hardest in the morning.
  • Anyone who wakes up to urgency, loose stool, or abdominal discomfort that feels unpredictable.
  • Those who tried diet changes but still have inconsistent mornings.
  • People who suspect timing matters but cannot see the full pattern.

Why timing matters

For many sensitive people, the gut–brain system reacts as much to timing and rhythm as it does to food. Late liquids, drifting magnesium timing, and inconsistent sleep compress gut activity into the early morning window.

This tool maps that pattern so the morning feels less like a coin flip.

What you can learn from this

  • How late liquids affect morning urgency.
  • Whether magnesium timing helps or hurts depending on the window.
  • How sleep consistency affects gut sensitivity.
  • Which timing combinations produce your calmest mornings.

What this tool is not

  • Not a medical diagnosis.
  • Not a diet or supplement program.
  • Not a replacement for medical care.
  • Not a product—just a free timing model.

FAQ

Does this replace diet work?

No. Diet matters. This is for people whose mornings remain unpredictable even after diet changes.

How long before I see a pattern?

Most people need 2–4 weeks of consistent timing to reveal clear trends. The nervous system responds to repetition.

Do you collect any data?

No. The calculator runs fully in your own browser. Nothing is uploaded.
