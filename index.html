How the timing calculator works

The goal is simple: show you when your evenings are setting you up for either a stable or chaotic morning, so it feels less random.

  1. Enter last night. You log when you ate dinner or late snacks, when you stopped liquids, when you took magnesium, and when you actually slept and woke up.
  2. Add context. Add your sugar, electrolytes, fiber, stress, and exercise for the day.
  3. Get a Morning Stability Score. The tool gives a score from 0 to 100 showing what helped or hurt your odds of a calm morning.

You are not being judged; you are just looking at rhythm instead of guessing every morning.

Why timing can matter more than “what you ate”

Most IBS advice is a food list. For sensitive people, there is often a different problem: the timing and the nervous system.

If you have visceral hypersensitivity, your system may react not just to what you ate, but to when your gut has to move water, magnesium, sugar, and waste through a sensitive colon relative to your sleep and wake cycle.

Why this tool exists

The creator of this tool spent years with IBS, visceral hypersensitivity, and chaotic mornings. Doctors suggested fiber, probiotics, stress reduction — nothing explained why some mornings were fine and others were chaos.

Once the pattern was framed as “liquids + magnesium + sleep timing + sensitivity,” mornings finally made sense. This project makes that timing model free for anyone to test.

Quick FAQ

No. It is a timing model, not a diagnosis.

No. The tool runs in your browser only.

How long should I try this?

Give it a few weeks. The nervous system learns through repetition, not one-night experiments.
